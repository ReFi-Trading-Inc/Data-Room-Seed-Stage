# Market Sizing & Opportunity Analysis

## Total Addressable Market (TAM): $18B

**Target Universe:** 15 million active retail algo traders globally

**Calculation:**
- Global retail trading accounts: ~200 million (2024)
- Active traders (20+ trades/year): 40% = 80 million
- Algo/automation interest: 30% = 24 million
- Serviceable with current infrastructure: 62.5% = 15 million
- At $100/month ARPU: 15M × $100 × 12 = **$18B TAM**

**Geographic Breakdown:**
- North America: 4.5M traders (30%) = $5.4B
- Europe: 3.0M traders (20%) = $3.6B
- APAC: 6.0M traders (40%) = $7.2B
- MENA/Other: 1.5M traders (10%) = $1.8B

## Serviceable Available Market (SAM): $600M

**Serious Algorithmic Traders:** 500,000 users

**Qualification Criteria:**
- Portfolio size: $10K-$500K
- Trading frequency: Weekly or more
- Technical proficiency: Basic coding or willingness to learn automation
- Current pain: Losing money to emotional trading or paying high fees to prop firms

**Calculation:**
- 500K serious algo traders
- At $100/month average ARPU: 500K × $100 × 12 = **$600M SAM**

**Segmentation:**
1. **Power Retail Traders** (350K) - 70%
   - $10K-$100K portfolios
   - DIY algo traders using TradingView scripts, Python bots
   - Primary segment for Basic/Pro tiers

2. **Small Fund Managers** (100K) - 20%
   - $100K-$500K under management
   - Currently using manual trading or basic automation
   - Target for Enterprise tier + performance fees

3. **Aspiring Quants** (50K) - 10%
   - STEM backgrounds transitioning to trading
   - High engagement, low AUM initially
   - Future growth segment

- 2024: 18M active US retail traders

- Projected 2028: 32M active US traders

**Algo Trading Adoption:**
- 2020: 8% of retail traders use automation

4. Post-pandemic digital-first investment behavior

## Risk Factors & Mitigation

**Market Risks:**
1. **Regulatory crackdown on algo trading:** Mitigated by ADGM license, non-custodial model
2. **Market crash reducing trading activity:** Mitigated by risk-off agents, hedging strategies
3. **Broker API shutdowns:** Mitigated by multi-broker redundancy via SnapTrade

**Competitive Risks:**
1. **3Commas/Cryptohopper pivot to non-custodial:** 18-24 month rebuild timeline, regulatory barriers
2. **IBKR/Questrade building native RL bots:** Not core competency, conflicts with brokerage model
3. **New entrants:** Patent-pending IP + regulatory moat + 12-month head start

**Adoption Risks:**
1. **Trust barrier for API access:** Mitigated by educational content, SOC 2, audit trails
2. **Technical complexity for retail:** Mitigated by one-click agent deployment, paper trading
3. **Performance skepticism:** Mitigated by transparent backtest data, live performance dashboard
